  • 114. IoT

    Category: Application Design

    Notes:

    Internet of Things (IoT) is a proliferation of smart objects (cars, home appliances, devices) that connect the physical world to the digital world. Humans and/or systems interact with these devices to achieve benefits such as (a) improved Business insights, (b) improved Customer experience, (c) efficiency and productivity gains, (d) asset tracking and waste reduction, and (e) help in identifying new Business models.

    IoT can be broadly classified into two categories based on its usage context;

    • Industrial IoT (IIoT)
              Defined as ‘machines, computers, and people enabling intelligent industrial operations using advanced data analytics for transformational business outcomes”.
    • Consumer IoT (CIoT)
              Defined as connected devices that are aimed to bring value to the consumer market. The CIoT applications can range from very simple ones as personal fitness devices to high-end home automation applications.

    More Info: swa.fyi/RA/iot

    Radar Contact: Steve.Taylor@wnco.com

    Popularized by ThoughtWorks, a global technology consultation company, a "Tech Radar" is a way of visualizing technology trends within the information technology industry. The Radar both assesses the current usefulness and viability of existing and legacy technologies, as well as forecasts future viability of emerging ones.

    ThoughtWorks produces their own interactive Tech Radar which shows how they classify technologies from a holistic, industry-wide perspective. Internally, many organizations produce their own Tech Radars, given the specific needs, restraints, and goals of individual enterprises which can differ from the wider technology industry.

    Within Southwest Airlines, Enterprise Architecture has developed own our Tech Radar, tailored to the Southwest Airlines IT Landscape. Once completed, the Radar will give easily-consumed guidance about what mix of technologies are best for our Enterprise, ensuring balance between adopting new technologies and retiring old ones where appropriate, and maintaining any current technology usage which is serving us well.

    Visit this page for more information on the tech radar.
